# South Holston — Plan B Waters

When TVA is releasing and the SoHo below the dam is too high/dangerous to wade (and you don't want to float), there are strong nearby options. All within a short drive.

## Watauga River

- Tailwater out of **Wilbur Dam**, running down to **Elizabethton, TN** — roughly 10 mi south of Bluff City via Hwy 19E.
- Cold year-round, excellent trout fishery — arguably **every bit as good as the SoHo**. Often fishable when the SoHo is releasing.
- BWO nymphs are a go-to (locals fish them on standard nymph hooks more than jigs).

## Doe River

- Flows north into Elizabethton along Hwy 19E. Fun to fish, and gets genuinely wild and serious where it runs through the **gorge** — wild fish in there.

## Beaver Dam Creek

- At and above **Backbone Rock** (state park, Hwy 133), in TN just under the VA line near Damascus. ~40 min from the Castaways area.
- **Wild slam** water — you can grand-slam wild fish here most trips. Backbone Rock itself is a novelty (a short tunnel bored through a rock spine).

## Dennis Cove Recreation Area / Laurel Fork Creek

- Southeast of **Hampton, TN** (below Elizabethton). Note: this Laurel Fork is **not** the one through Damascus, VA — different creek, similar name.
- A genuinely great wild stream — source angler caught his most beautiful, dark-blood-red rainbow here.
- **Caution:** snakes, including **copperheads** — mind the season and watch your step.

## Holston Mountains streams

- The ~4,200 ft Holston Mountains south of the river hold water on both faces. Left deliberately vague by the source (no hot-spotting) — worth exploring on your own.

## Why bother when the SoHo is "a sure thing"

Catching fish on the SoHo is so reliable that a few days of it can get monotonous — the plan-B wild streams are where you go to actually hunt: sneak up on a six-inch brookie that's never seen a human and trick it. Different, harder, satisfying.
