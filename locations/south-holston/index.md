# South Holston River (SoHo) — East Tennessee

A TVA tailwater below South Holston Dam, widely rated one of the best trout fisheries in the eastern US. Cold year-round, extraordinary insect biomass, and a lot of big, well-fed, well-educated fish.

**Type:** tailwater (bottom-release) · **Species:** wild browns, rainbows, plus big carp
**Region:** East Tennessee — Bluff City up to South Holston Dam; nickname "SoHo"
**Local nickname for the fishing:** "assisted living" — flat, wadeable, and stacked with fish

{% include release-panel.html %}

## Why it fishes the way it does

- **~14.6 miles** of river from the dam down to Island Park at Bluff City (where it enters Boone Lake).
- **Barely any gradient** — drops only ~137 ft over 14.6 mi (well under 1%). Flat, wadeable terrain the whole way.
- **Fish density is the headline:** locals cite ~8,500 fish/mile → on the order of 125,000 fish in that stretch.
- **Food everywhere:** turn over any rock and it's crawling — midges, mayflies (sulfurs, BWO), stoneflies, scuds, caddis. Fish are big because the water stays cold and the buffet never closes.
- **Clear, technical water, tea-colored tint.** These fish see heavy pressure and get cautious. Small flies and a careful approach matter (see [flies.md](flies.md)).

## The three sections (local terminology)

The river breaks into three stretches bottom-to-top. This is how the source anglers divide it — outfitters may use different names. Detail in [access-and-sections.md](access-and-sections.md).

1. **Lower** — ~4.25 mi, Island Park (Bluff City) up to the Riverside Rd bridge.
2. **Mid** — ~5.4 mi, up to the Hickory Tree Rd / Hwy 44 bridge.
3. **Upper** — ~5 mi, up to the dam. Holds the Big Bend public-land stretch and the weir dams.
