# South Holston — Releases & Safety

**Read this before you wade.** SoHo is a TVA bottom-release tailwater. When they generate, the water comes up fast and hard — from wadeable riffles to dangerous current in minutes. People get hurt underestimating it.

## How to check the schedule

- Google **"TVA South Holston River release schedule"** → the TVA site → pick South Holston / Holston River.
- **TVA has an app** (same one works for the Toccoa in GA and other TVA tailwaters) — recommended over checking a laptop.
- **Schedules change, often.** Treat the posted schedule as a plan, not a guarantee. Check the day of, and again before you commit to a long wade downstream.

## Timing — how fast it reaches you

- Water travels down the ~14.6 miles at a predictable pace: from the dam, the surge takes **~4 hours to reach the bottom** (Bluff City / Castaways area, ~14 mi down).
- The number of generation tubes running (you can often see 3–4 going below the dam) drives how big and fast the surge is.

## Warnings on the water

- **Sirens** sound at the dam and on Osceola Island when they release — but once you're well downstream you **won't hear them.** Don't rely on the siren; rely on the schedule and on watching the water.
- Signs, flashing lights, and sirens are posted up and down the river. Still: know your schedule and know where you are relative to your exit.
- Easy to lose track of time while fishing and get caught. Give yourself margin.

## Fishing the release (it doesn't kill the bite)

- Fish are **used to** daily up-and-down — the release doesn't put them off feeding.
- **Back end preferred:** as the water drops off after a release, bug activity is strong and the fishing is often excellent. There's usually activity on the front end (rising water) too.
- **In a drift boat it doesn't matter** — you fish through it fine, sometimes catching as many or more, and bigger, than the waders.
- **Plan B:** when the whole lower/mid stretch is blown out by a release and you don't want to float, jump to a nearby water — see [plan-b-waters.md](plan-b-waters.md).

## Regulations tied to timing

- **Brown trout spawn closures:** a couple of sections close during the spawn. Regardless of closures, leave spawning browns alone anywhere you find them — let them reproduce. Fish **egg patterns** for the rainbows keying on the eggs (see [flies.md](flies.md)).
- **Felt ban:** reportedly may be banned in TN/VA/WV at some point — verify current regs; rubber soles (e.g. Korkers Aluma-Trax) are the hedge.
