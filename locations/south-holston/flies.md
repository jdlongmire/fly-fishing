# South Holston — Flies & Rigging

The through-line: **fish small.** Over half a SoHo trout's diet is midges, and size 20–22 is the norm, sometimes smaller. Flies are a confidence game — fish what you trust — but on this technical, clear, hard-pressured water, small + light tippet is what earns eats.

> Bead color is a real variable here. Rule of thumb: **silver** on bright/sunny days (mimics the emerger air sac), **gold** in stained/mucky water, **copper** as the third option. If silver's dead, switch to gold, then copper.

## Midges (the staple)

| Pattern | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Zebra midge (brown/copper) | 20–22 | Locals call it a "sweet thing." Fish love zebras. |
| Purple zebra midge, silver bead | 20 | Sleeper — out-fishes standard midges when others are struggling. |
| White midge / "miracle midge" | 20 | White thread body; clear bead rides it up in the film. Turn a rock — you'll see white midges. |
| Matt's midge | 20 | Black or red thread, trailing shuck with a single strand of crystal flash. |

## Mayflies

| Pattern | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| BWO (jig nymph) | 20 | Dominating bug. Bead color by water clarity (gold when stained). A near-necessity here and on the Watauga. |
| Split case (BWO / sulfur) | ~18–20 | A favorite — ties in 2–3 min. Fish it anytime sulfurs are around. |
| Sulfur dry (Adams sulfur, or Mack Daddy) | 12–20 | Big sulfur hatches, especially lower river; some seasons the bugs run large (12–14). |
| Frenchie | 16 | Jig hook, hot spot on tail and collar. Silver/purple version fishes well in winter. |

## Caddis & soft hackles

| Pattern | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Mack Daddy (CDC caddis) | ~big | About the only caddis fished here — floats enormously (treat with Loon Lochsa / Frog's Fanny), easy to see in riffles, and will hold a heavy dropper (size 10–12) without sinking. Fish will even eat the indicator; also tied pink/white. |
| Puff Daddy | — | Similar to the Mack Daddy; popular locally. |
| Stinger soft hackle (own pattern) | 10–12 | Lead under the dubbing — gets down **fast** as a point fly. Orange / lime-green combo fishes "like crazy." |
| CDC / pheasant-tail soft hackle | — | Saman camo thread body; olive/red/copper. Gold bead if water's mucky. |
| Soft-hackle emerger | 20 | Weir-dam area. Tied on a dry hook (wider gap), **unweighted** — fishes as an emerger. |

## Dries & dusk

| Pattern | Size | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Poly-wing midge / F-fly | 20 | Old pattern (50–60 yrs). Excellent **brown trout** bug as the sun drops / dusk settles; not a rainbow magnet. Also a Davidson River / Blue Ridge Parkway producer. |
| Elk Hair Caddis | — | Sam's confidence dry. |

## Scuds — don't ignore the shade

Lots of grass = lots of scuds. **Green vs. tan matters more than you'd think.** A tan scud that had been producing went dead; a barely-greener scud drew a fish first and second cast. Carry both shades and switch.

## Eggs

During the brown spawn, rainbows key on eggs — **carry egg patterns.**

## Rigging & tippet

- **Nymph rig:** heavier point fly to punch down fast, drop **tungsten** off it. Runs stay deep/fast even on low water — the faster you're down, the better.
- **Nymphing rod:** a 10–11 ft 3 wt euro/nymph rod for those runs.
- **Tippet:** the SoHo Fly Shop pushes **7X** (very technical water). A practical middle ground is 6.5X (e.g. Trout Hunter) for a little insurance on a good fish; 6X still catches plenty if you fish your drag right. **Bamboo** buys a bit of extra tippet protection via its softer flex.
- **Watch the birds:** small finch-like birds swarming and hitting the surface = a hatch is on. Switch to midges fast — the window turns on and off quickly.
