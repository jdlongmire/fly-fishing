# South Holston — Logistics

Lodging, food, outfitters, and getting there. Verify hours/availability — these change.

## Outfitters

- **South Holston River Fly Shop** — the source anglers' primary connection. Matt Champion (2nd-generation owner) is regarded as one of the best guides on the water. Also sells a good river map worth grabbing.
- **Eastern Fly Outfitters** — strong fly-tying selection.
- **The Fly Box** — newer shop, reportedly near the weirs, with **beer taps** (per a viewer).

## Places to stay

- **Castaways** ("Holston Castaways") — where the source anglers usually stay; ~100 yards from the river, walk right down to it. Not the only option but a solid base.
- **South Holston River Cabins** — cabins on the water.
- **Papa's** — sits right at the bottom of the Big Springs Rd bridge, on the water; rentable.
- **South Holston River Lodge** — high-end (out of most budgets), but does it right: good chefs, wine-forward.
- **Cherokee Trails Campground** (and other area campgrounds) for camping.
- Chain hotels (e.g. Hampton Inn) a few miles off if you'd rather.
- General tip: Google "South Holston River," drill to river level on the map, and it highlights the rentals/B&Bs along the water — most are reasonably priced.

## Food (Bluff City area)

- **Breakfast / chicken-fried steak spot** in Bluff City — one of the best breakfasts around (reduced days due to cutbacks — check hours).
- **Ridgewood Barbecue** — legendary portions; the standard sandwich can defeat a grown adult. Big barbecue reputation.
- **Big Dan's** — moved off the river into downtown, next to the local **tap house** (nice outdoor area, good beer selection).

## Getting there

- **Airport:** Tri-Cities Airport (serves Kingsport / Bristol / Johnson City) for commercial. Several smaller GA fields around for private (172 up to a Citation).
- **Roads:** I-81 (NE–SW past Bristol, between Kingsport & Johnson City), I-26 (up from Asheville through Johnson City / Kingsport), Hwy 19E (from Boone / Banner Elk NC), Hwy 421 (Boone up to Bristol). Easy to reach — get to Johnson City and hop onto 19.
- **Watch your speed.** Local enforcement is active — if the sign says 45, do 45.

## Private-pilot note

If you fly in yourself, respect **Holston Mountain** (~4,200 ft) — the local weather patterns pile up fog and IFR conditions against it. (Source anglers cite a 1976 fatal F-4 crash into the mountain as the cautionary tale.) Set your barometer, fly the plan.
