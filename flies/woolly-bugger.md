# Woolly Bugger

**Type:** Streamer (fishes as leech / baitfish / large nymph)
**Imitates:** leeches, baitfish, crayfish, big stonefly nymphs — a searching pattern more than an exact match
**Sizes I carry:** #6–12
**Colors:** black, olive, brown; add a little flash or a bead head for deeper/faster water

## When it produces

Nearly year-round and nearly everywhere — the classic confidence pattern. Especially strong in off-color water, low light, and when nothing is hatching and you need to cover water and provoke a reaction.

## How to fish it

Several ways, all worth trying:
- **Stripped:** cast across or down-and-across, let it sink, retrieve in short strips. Vary speed until a fish tells you.
- **Swung:** cast down-and-across, let it swing on a tight line through the seam; takes come hard at the end of the swing.
- **Dead-drifted:** under an indicator like a heavy nymph, bounced along the bottom.

Bead-head or weighted versions get down in current; unweighted for shallow or stillwater.

## Tying notes

Marabou tail, chenille body, palmered saddle hackle; optional bead head and a few strands of Krystal Flash in the tail. One of the first flies most tyers learn — forgiving of imperfection.
