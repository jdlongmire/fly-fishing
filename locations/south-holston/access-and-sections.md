# South Holston — Access & Sections

Roads parallel nearly the entire river, so access is rarely the problem. The river divides into three sections bottom-to-top. Mileages are approximate and follow the source anglers' terminology.

## Lower section (~4.25 mi)

From **Island Park** at Bluff City (where the river enters Boone Lake) up to the **Riverside Rd bridge**.

- Down near Island Park it's **deeper — better floated** now that Boone Lake runs full.
- Moving up you get great **shoals and shelves**, very clear, technical water — sneak up on the fish.
- **J. Forest Thomas access** — boat ramp roughly halfway up the lower section. Put in / take out here down to Island Park, or park and wade; classic shoal-and-run structure right there, another shoal every ~50 yards.
- Pockets of deep water in the runs sometimes hold **big carp** (25–35"+) lying on the bottom — hook one and it fights like a snagged log, straight to your backing. Needs more than a 4–6 wt to horse out.

## Mid section (~5.4 mi)

From the Riverside Rd bridge up to the **Hickory Tree Rd / Hwy 44 bridge**. Big Springs Rd runs parallel much of the way.

- Largely wadeable when there's no release, with some deep holes that want a drift boat.
- Residential along the south side; gorgeous water. You can spot schools of 30–40 big trout (and carp) holding — extremely sensitive fish, fish them carefully.
- **Hwy 44 boat launch** about halfway through the mid section (where the frontage road turns 90° to Hwy 44).
- Frontage-road turnouts roughly **every 50 yards** to park and wade the shoals and runs.
- Heavy shoal structure ~1.5 mi up; deepest water ~4 ft against the outside banks, wadeable shoals on the inside. Pasture land alongside → good **terrestrial** fishing in summer (hoppers).

## Upper section (~5 mi)

From the Hwy 44 bridge up to the **dam**. The most scenic and least-pressured water.

- **Big Bend** — ~1.5 mi up from the bridge, past an island, into a stretch of **public land** (rare on this river — most banks are private farmland buffered by trees). Flanked by tall hills on one side, hay pasture on the other. Almost no traffic; you won't see other anglers this far down. Flat, spread-out, wadeable when not releasing.
  - **Access is on foot.** Trail down, or hike/wade from the dam side. A viewer noted: **end of River Bend Rd** has a parking area, ~½-mile hike in, loop trail.
- **Osceola Island** — ~1 mile long, laced with trails, footbridge across (look down and you'll see trout even when they're not biting). The **weir dams** sit on both sides of the island.
- **Weir dams** — accordion/reticulated structures ~8–9 ft tall that increase surface area for aeration; normally only a foot or two of water at the base. You can **wade and fish up into them** with water falling on both sides. Great fishing from the weirs down to the aeration grids, under the bridge, and into Big Bend.
- **Where the crowds are:** from the weirs down to the Holston View Down Rd area is where you'll see most anglers, especially locals. Kayak/boat launch by the bridge (a couple of put-in options; watch a ~1 ft concrete drop on one side).
- **The dam** — ~200+ ft high. Overflow spillway plus generation tubes (most water released here except in high water). Aeration grids below. You can drive across the top — worth the look.

## Floating & put-ins

- The river is **floatable top to bottom**, especially during releases; a few spots need a pull-over at low water.
- Put in / take out roughly **every 2–2.5 miles** all the way down to Island Park (or on into Boone Lake).

## Wading note

Bottom is rocky with grass beds; felt usually suffices here (see [logistics.md](logistics.md) / gear). The real hazard isn't the rock — it's the **release**. See [release-schedule.md](release-schedule.md).
